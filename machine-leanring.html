XGBoost for Wave Reflection, Transmission, and Overtopping Prediction

Machine learning ENTHUSIAST
|
XGBoot

As coastal engineers, we are consistently dealing with wave-structure interaction processes such as wave reflection, wave transmission, and wave overtopping. Wave reflection in harbors can cause resonances, which enhance wave energy and put the coastal structures at risk. Wave transmission determines how much wave energy can penetrate through breakwaters therefore controls the wave energy approaching the shoreline. Wave overtopping poses directly thread to the properties behind coastal defenses. Accurate estimation of those processes is critical for the design of coastal and harbor structures and the assessment of their hydraulic performances.
This project is to apply the XGBoost algorithm for the estimation of the key parameters related to wave-structure interaction using a homogeneous database compiled by Zanuttigh and Van der Meer (2014) from two EU projects DELOS and CLASH with some additional dataset. The project is aiming to develop a model for accurate prediction of wave reflection and transmission coefficients and wave overtopping discharge, which will enable coastal engineers to design better coastal and harbor structures. DeepTC

Machine learning ENTHUSIAST
|
Deep LeARNING - CNN, LSTM, GAN, ATTENTION

Tropical Cyclones, hereafter referred to as TCs, are severe weather system originated over warm tropical ocean. They are known as hurricanes in the Atlantic and Northeast Pacific, cyclones in the South Pacific and Indian Ocean, and typhoons in the Northwest Pacific. TCs typically move from east to west due to the tropical trade winds near the equator where the TCs originate, and curve into higher latitude as a result of the Coriolis force and steering winds at higher elevation. Many TCs made direct landfall, which caused significant threat to life and property. Although the westerly winds prevail at higher altitude may steer the storm back to the east into the ocean and some of them may be weakened to a tropical storm or become extratropical, TCs can still produce hurricane-force winds and high storm surge along the coast even remained offshore. For the TC warning and disaster management, the prediction of the intensity as well as the track of the TCs is very important for the local authorities in their decision making regarding the time and location of evacuation to reduce the potential damage due to the TCs.
This project is to explore and develop deep neural network to: accurately estimate the intensity of the TCs based on the satellite images; to forecast the track of the TCs from the advisory information (location, maximum speed, radius etc.); and ultimately, to forecast the track and characteristics of the TCs based on the satellite images which is called deepTC.

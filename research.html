Shear Flow along Partially Vegetated Bank

REsearch Assistant
|
coastal engineering

This is one of Aaron's research topic at North Carolina State University funded by NSF. The main research objective is to develop a numerical model to simulate the laboratory shear flow in a partially vegetated channel conducted by Nepf's group at MIT. The model solves shallow water equations with Finite Volume Method, which is characterized by approximate HLLC Riemann solver and MUSCL-Hancock time integration. Compared with laboratory study by Nepf, the model well captured the development of shear flow in terms of the momentum thickness, dominant frequency, as well as the equilibrium momentum profile and vortex structure. Hydrodynamic Instability Mechanism for Rip Currents

REsearch Assistant
|
Applied MATHEMATICS

This is Aaron's primary research topic at North Carolina State University funded by NSF. The research objective is to investigate the generation mechanism of rip current due to hydrodynamic instability using a mathematical analysis approach. Yu (2006) showed that the positive feedback between the wave and evolving current can initiate and sustain rip current circulations with scales comparable to field observations on alongshore uniform beaches, when the fully dynamical interactions are accounted for, and proper mathematical analysis is carried out. Aaron extended that analysis to consider non-planar beaches and identified a new branch of unstable modes that correspond to alongshore propagating horizontal circulations with the magnitudes of the flow growing in time. This has not previously been studied. These propagating unstable modes have typical time periods of tens of minutes and alongshore propagation speeds of a few centimeters per second, which is relevance to occurrence and recurrence of transient rips, alongshore migration of rip currents and very low frequency pulsations in surf zone eddy circulations. CurWav-Sed

software developer
|
Numerical modeling

This is Aaron's primary research topic at Tongji University. The objective is to develop a state of art numerical modelling system for current wave coupled hydrodynamic and sediment transport model based on a semi-implicit Eulerian-Lagrangian Finite Volume Method. The mesh module handles the triangular grid in Euler System, on which shallow water equations are solved. The convection term is treated using Lagrangian approach and the hydrostatic pressure is handled semi-implicitly, which is free of numerical diffusion and unconditionally stable. The resulting linear system with sparse coefficient matrix is solved by JCG method. The current module is coupled with wave module SWAN in parallel using the concept of communication file. The convection and diffusion equation for passive substances such as temperature, salinity, sediment etc. are solved by Finite Volume Method and are consistent with the current module. The model is fully validated and successfully applied to several projects, including salinity intrusion study in Yangtz River and sediment transport modeling for a reclamation project in Bohai Bay.

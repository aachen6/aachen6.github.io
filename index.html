About me

Mr Chen is a highly motivated coastal and environmental engineer with solid background in estuarine and coastal dynamics, computational fluid dynamics, coastal processes, shoreline restoration, and coastal structure design. He is specialized in developing and/or using numerical models to study wave transformation, hydrodynamics (circulation, storm surge, flooding), and the associated coastal processes such as sediment transport, morphological changes, and pollutant migration. Mr Chen is also specialized in time series analysis and statistical analysis of the single or multi-variable extreme values. In addition, he has rich experiences in hydrographic surveys and geographic mapping. Recently, Mr. Chen develops a special interest in machine learning and is initiating a few projects applying that to tropical storm tracking.

2010-2014

North Carolina State University

Research assistant
|

Aaron was a research assistant during his graduate study in North Carolina State University. His primary research topic, funded by NSF, is to investigate the generation mechanism of rip currents due to hydrodynamic instability (versus traditionally bathymetry controlled rips). Aaron applied mathematical approach to identify potential positive feedback between dynamic interaction of wave and current that can initiate and sustain rip current circulations on non-planar beaches. He obtained a branch of unstable modes solution similar to what was previously derived on planar beaches. He also identified a new branch of unstable modes that correspond to alongshore propagating horizontal circulations with the magnitudes of the flow growing in time, which has not previously been studied. Aaron also developed a numerical model to verify the results derived from the mathematical analysis. In addition, he used the model successfully reproduce numerically the laboratory study of shear flow in a partially vegetated open channel conducted at MIT. Tongji University

Research assistant

Teaching assistant

Aaron was research and teaching assistant during his graduate study in Tongji University. His primary research topic is to develop a state of art numerical modelling system for current wave coupled hydrodynamic and sediment transport model based on a semi-implicit Eulerian-Lagrangian Finite Volume Method. The model features: unstructured triangular mesh; 2nd order accuracy with minimal numerical diffusion; and unconditionally stable schema allowing large time step to enhance computational efficiency. Aaron calibrated the model by examining different components of the model using theoretical and experiment studies for pure convection flows, pure diffusion processes, wetting and drying schema, morphological changes etc. During his research, Aaron also led the numerical modelling aspects for a few engineering projects, including hydro- and morpho-dynamic evaluation of Caofeidian near harbor industrial zone, salinity intrusion in Yangtze River, flood risk mapping along Taipu River.

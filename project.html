Design of Shore Protection for Tottenville

ASSISTANT MANAGER
|
COASTAL ENGINEERING

This project is to design a mile long shore protection structures with the objective to reduce wave action and coastal erosion while also enhancing ecosystems and beach access along the shoreline of Tottenville, NY. The design includes five unique segments, i.e. earthen berm, wetland eco-revetment, hybrid sand dune, eco-revetment, and raised edge pathway. Aaron worked as assistant manager for coastal analysis and engineering design. Specifically, he supervised the 1-D analysis of erosion, runup and overtopping calculation, and overland wave propagation. Aaron also led the 2-D phase-resolving wave modelling efforts, design of typical sections for five unique design segments and the corresponding analysis of wave forces and rocksize. He is currently working on the basis of design report, specs, and bid package. Read more...

Storm Surge and Wave Modeling for Lake Michigan

technical lead
|
FEMA, COASTAL STORM SURGE

As part of a joint venture contracted with FEMA to address the issues concerning imprecise offshore wave modeling applied in recent flood studies for Lake Michigan, we were scoped to hindcast 150 historical storms by developing a coupled storm surge and wave model using ADCIRC+SWAN for Lake Michigan. The model was driven by the lakewide wind and pressure fields and considered the effects of ice coverage for winter storms. Aaron was technical lead for this efforts. He led a team of engineers developed and calibrated the model against measurements from NOAA and NDBC bouys. 150 historical storms since 1960s were identified and hindcasted by the model. This modelling effort provided accurate water levels and nearshore waves associated with those historical storm events, which were applied to FEMA county-wide coastal Physical Map Revision (PMR) projects throughout the entire shoreline of Lake Michigan. Sensitivity of Random Tide Selection on Storm Surge Elevation

technical lead
|
FEMA, COASTAL STORM SURGE

NYC challenged the recent FEMA preliminary map for NJ/NY issued in 2015. One particular concern arising from the random tide selection for each storm event, in particular extratropical storms, which may lead to overestimation of the percent-annual-chance water level (PWL). We were scoped to evaluate the sensitivity of the PWL to the random tide selection and the method to minimize the aleatory bias due to such sensitivity. Aaron led the investigation efforts. He examined the peak storm surge elevation as a function of its occurrence time along a tide signal and the corresponding nonlinearity. Based on the investigation of the nonlinearity, Aaron used a Monte-Carlo simulation approach and determined the PWL variations to the random tide selection and the number of random tides needed to reduce the aleatory bias. FEMA Coastal Flood Insurance PMR, Region V and X

Coastal and software ENGINEER
|
FEMA, COASTAL flood hazard

As FEMA contractor, we were scoped for county-wide coastal flood hazard analysis and delineation of flood risk map for six counties in FEMA Region X, twelve counties in Lake Michigan, and seven counties in Lake Huron. Aaron was coastal and software engineer and developed a package of codes for engineering analysis that scales to hundreds of transects and enables a detail analysis and mapping of flood risk. Tasks automated by the code package include: interactive runup feature identification; storm extraction from 2-D model; parallel CSHORE erosion analysis; runup and overtopping analysis using various empirical methods; joint probability analysis for event based assessment; automated WHAFIS inland wave propagation analysis; and robust statistical analysis (maximum likelihood fitting of Generalized Extreme Value Distribution or Generalized Pareto Distribution with Q-Q optimization). Aaron also performed the analyses and delineated floodplain boundaries using ArcGIS per FEMA guideline for Island County, WA, Clallam County, WA, Cook County, IL, Lake County, IL, Lake County IN, Alcona County, MI, Iosco County, MI, and Huron County, MI.

Living Shoreline of West Pond

technical lead
|
shoreline restoration

The West Pond in Jamaica Bay, NY was breached during Sandy. We were scoped by NPS to design a living shoreline on the bay side to restore the fresh water pond. Aaron led the coastal analysis component of the project. Specifically, Aaron analyzed the wind conditions at the site, and performed the fetch analysis to determine the annual wave power. Based on the annual wave power, he calculated the marsh edge erosion rate under normal condition. Aaron also performed statistical analysis of water level and wind to determine the storm conditions of different re-occurrence intervals and produced the corresponding wave fields using SWAN. Based on the wave analysis, Aaron determined the design storm event and the most damaging wave direction and evaluated the wave attenuation effects for four design alternatives, which informed the selection of the design alternatives. COASTAL ENGINEERING

This project is to evaluate the hydro- and morpho-dynamic impact due to the construction of a near harbor industrial zone in Bohai Bay, which involves a large-scale land reclamation reaching up to 310 km2.  Aaron performed time series analysis of current, wave, and suspended sediment to examine their characteristics. He also developed and calibrated a current wave coupled sediment transport model using CurWav2D-Sed that he developed, and performed simulations for different phases of the construction.

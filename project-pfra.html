Probabilistic Flood Risk Assessment of the Great Lakes

FEMA is moving from the deterministic approach for flood risk assessment to the probabilistic flood risk assessment (PFRA). Aaron serves as the task lead for the Great Lakes with members across all FEMA subcontractors. Specifically, he leads the effort for extensive region-wide statistical analysis along the shoreline of the Great Lakes looking at the behaviors of the extreme value distribution, uncertainty assessment, the influence of the lake levels on the recurrence frequency of the stillwater level, joint probability among different forcing variables for overland wave hazards analysis. Based on the observations from those analyses, he proposed the methodology for the PFRA of the Great Lakes addressing challenges like influence of lake level variability in the storm surge modeling, combined flood risk due to storm surge and wave runup and overtopping, fast generation of map risk maps for multiple return periods with runup and overtopping hazards for the probabilistic risk evaluation.
